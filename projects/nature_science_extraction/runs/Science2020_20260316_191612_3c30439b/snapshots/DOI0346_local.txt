Source and release of arsenic in groundwater in MGP No specific sources of arsenic could be identified from MGP. Groundwater arsenic contamination is also recorded from Terai belt in Nepal (Gurung et al. 2005). The char- acter, nature and age of contaminated aquifers from Terai basin of Nepal (Himalayan foothill) are closely comparable to those of MGP. The provenance of the Terai sediments is the Himalayan hill range. Moreover, bulk of sediments discharged from the Himalayan hill range are also accu- mulated in the Indo-Ganga foredeep basin (Fig. 6). Arsenic contaminated areas are confined within this basin. Depo- sitional pattern of sediments and fluvial facies in MGP are closely comparable with those in Bengal Basin (Umitsu 1993; Singh 2001). Conclusions The major parts of the MGP (90%) consists of inter-fluve upland terraces (T2-Surface) of Older Alluvium. These oxidizing Pleistocene upland terraces are well flushed by groundwater flow due to high-hydraulic head and are generally free of arsenic contamination in groundwater. The Newer Alluvium (Holocene) is characterized by or- ganic rich grey to black coloured argillaceous sediments.
