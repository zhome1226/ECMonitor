ABSTRACT: Perﬂuorinated chemicals (PFCs) are emerging environmental contaminants with a global distribution. Due to their moderate water solubility, the majority of the environmental burden is assumed to be in the water phase. This work describes the application of the ﬁrst passive sampler for the quantitative assessment of concentrations of perﬂuorinated alkylcarboxylates (PFCAs) and sulfonates (PFSAs) in water. The sampler is based on a modiﬁed Polar Organic Chemical Integrative Sampler (POCIS) with a weak anion exchange sorbent as a receiving phase. Sampling rates were between 0.16 and 0.37 L d−1, and the duration of the kinetic sampling stage was between 2.2 and 13 d. A ﬁeld deployment in the most urbanized estuary in Australia (Sydney Harbour) showed trace level concentrations from passive samplers (0.1−12 ng L−1), in good agreement with parallel grab sampling (0.2−16 ng L−1). A separate ﬁeld comparison of the modiﬁed POCIS with standard POCIS suggests the latter may have application for PFC sampling, but with a more limited range of analytes than the modiﬁed POCIS which contains a sorbent with a mixed mode of action. ■ INTRODUCTION Poly- and perﬂuoroalkyl compounds (PFCs) have been in the eﬄuent.13,14 This makes WWTP outfalls potential point sources in the environment.15,16 This tendency to pass through manufactured on an industrial scale for over 50 years.1,2 The water treatment also extends to drinking water, and numerous perﬂuoroalkylcarboxylates (PFCAs) and sulfonates (PFSAs) studies have found low level (ng L−1) concentrations in have surfactant properties, are anionic at the pH of environ- municipal drinking waters.17,18 In some areas contaminated mental waters, and resistant to both chemical and metabolic water supplies have provided a signiﬁcant contribution to daily degradation.3−5 The persistence of these compounds in the intakes and raised serum concentrations in residents environment, their consistent detection in monitoring stud- aﬀected.8,19,20 ies,6,7 and possible impact on organisms8 have prompted Passive sampling provides a low-cost and time-integrative growing concern.
