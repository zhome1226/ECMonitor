Previous studies have shown that organic surfactant enriched in SML can be transferred to marine aerosols that are subsequently transported remotely in the atmosphere (20, 29). Although its significance is still unclear, it is logical to consider that this may also be happening to PFCs. Given our evidence of different EFs for different compounds in the SML, it seems likely that the potential for marine aerosol transport would be different for each compound considered. Those surfactants that exhibit higher EFs would also have greater possibility of transport through this mechanism. The enrichment factors estimated from this study will be useful for modelers interested in estimating the importance of marine aerosols as a global transport process.
